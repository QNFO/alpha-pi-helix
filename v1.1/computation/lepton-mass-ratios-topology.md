## 3. Approach 2: Knot Theory

### Hypothesis
Each lepton is a knotted vortex of different complexity:
- $e$: unknot (trivial, crossing number c=0)
- $\mu$: trefoil knot ($c=3$)
- $\tau$: cinquefoil knot ($c=5$)

### Knot Energy Scaling
Knot energies scale approximately as $E \propto c^{3/2}$ for prime knots [established]. With $c = \{0,3,5\}$:
- Predicted $\mu/e$: $(3/1)^{3/2} \approx 5.2$ (observed: 207) ❌
- With an exponential scaling $m \propto e^{\kappa c}$:
  - $\kappa = \ln(206.8)/3 \approx 1.78$
  - Predicted $\tau/e$: $e^{5\kappa} = e^{8.9} \approx 7330$ (observed: 3477) — factor 2 off.

The exponential ansatz gets closer but still fails quantitatively.

## 4. Approach 3: Logarithmic Scale Separation

### Hypothesis
The mass hierarchy arises from the logarithmic running between the Planck scale and the electroweak scale, with different generations having different anomalous dimensions.

### RG Running
In the Standard Model, Yukawa couplings run:
$$\frac{d y_\ell}{d\ln\mu} = \frac{y_\ell}{16\pi^2}\left[ \frac{3}{2}y_\ell^2 + \cdots \right]$$

For small Yukawa couplings, running is weak. For large couplings (τ), running is significant. This suggests the hierarchy may be partially generated by RG running from a common high-scale value.

### Vortex RG
In the vortex model, the running of the effective mass with scale corresponds to the scale dependence of the vortex circulation:
$$\Gamma(\mu) = \Gamma(M_P) \cdot \left[1 + C \cdot \alpha(\mu) \cdot \ln\frac{\mu}{M_P}\right]$$

If different generations have different coefficients $C_n$, the masses at low energy can differ by factors of $10^2$-$10^3$ even if they are degenerate at the Planck scale.

## 5. Approach 4: Flavor Symmetry Breaking

### Hypothesis
At high energies ($E \gg m_\tau$), the three lepton vortices form a symmetric triplet (SO(3) or SU(3) flavor symmetry). As energy decreases, the symmetry breaks sequentially, producing the mass hierarchy.

### Symmetry Breaking Pattern
- **Stage 1** (GUT scale $\sim 10^{16}$ GeV): SO(3) → SO(2) — the τ vortex separates
- **Stage 2** (TeV scale): SO(2) → nothing — μ and e vortices separate

The mass ratios are then determined by the ratio of symmetry-breaking scales:
$$\frac{m_\tau}{m_\mu} \sim \frac{M_{GUT}}{M_{EW}} \sim \frac{10^{16}}{10^2} \sim 10^{14}$$

This is enormously too large. The symmetry-breaking scales do NOT directly map to mass ratios.

## 7. Honest Assessment

### What We Know
1. The mass ratios 1:207:3478 are NOT simple integers or rational numbers
2. No single topological invariant (winding number, crossing number, helicity) produces these ratios
3. The same α for all leptons means the aspect ratio is NOT the source of hierarchy

### What We Suspect
1. The hierarchy is a **low-energy phenomenon** — the fundamental theory has equal masses for all generations
2. The splitting is generated by **renormalization group running** from the unification scale
3. The vortex model provides the framework (Compton-scale geometry) but the actual mass values require the full QFT

### What Would Disconfirm
- If a future theory derived the exact mass ratios from pure topology (no RG running), the vortex model's reliance on RG would be falsified
- If all three lepton masses were found to be equal at the Planck scale via precision unification measurements, that would confirm the RG scenario
