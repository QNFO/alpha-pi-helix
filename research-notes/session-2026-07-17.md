## Research Trajectory

### Historical Foundations (Phase 1)
1. **Pi symbol origin:**
   - William Jones (1675–1749), Welsh mathematician
   - *Synopsis Palmariorum Matheseos* (1706): first use of π for circle constant
   - Chosen as abbreviation of περιφέρεια (periphereia, "circumference")
   - Earlier proto-uses: Oughtred (1647) used π for periphery of a specific circle
   - Euler standardized and popularized the notation
   - Source: Wikipedia (William Jones, Pi), MacTutor History of Mathematics

2. **Attic numeral system:**
   - Active ~7th century BCE to ~3rd century BCE
   - Acrophonic: symbols derived from first letters of number words
   - Π (𐅃) = 5 from ΠΕΝΤΕ [pente] "five"
   - Δ = 10 from ΔΕΚΑ [deka] "ten"
   - Η = 100 from ΗΕΚΑΤΟΝ [hekaton] "hundred"
   - Χ = 1000 from ΧΙΛΙΟΙ [khilioi] "thousand"
   - Μ = 10000 from ΜΥΡΙΟΙ [myrioi] "ten thousand"
   - Composites: 𐅄 = 50 (Δ inside Π), 𐅅 = 500 (Η inside Π)
   - Source: Wikipedia (Attic numerals)

3. **Pre-1706 circle constant:**
   - Verbal descriptions: "ratio of circumference to diameter"
   - Fractional approximations: 22/7 (Archimedes), 355/113 (Zu Chongzhi)
   - Proto-symbols: Oughtred's π/δ (periphery/diameter for specific circles)
   - Barrow used π as variable for perimeter
   - No universal symbol existed before Jones

### Mathematical Foundations (Phase 2)
4. **α = re / ƛ — Verified: EXACT identity**
   - re = e²/(4πε₀mc²)
   - ƛ = ħ/(mc)
   - α = e²/(4πε₀ħc)
   - re/ƛ = [e²/(4πε₀mc²)] / [ħ/(mc)] = e²/(4πε₀ħc) = α ✓
   - Also: α = 2πre/λc (since λc = 2πƛ)
   - Source: Wikipedia (Classical electron radius, Fine-structure constant)

5. **Full scaling chain:**
   - re = αƛ
   - ƛ = αa₀
   - a₀ = re/α²
   - All three fundamental electron length scales locked by α
   - Source: Wikipedia (Classical electron radius)

6. **Mass-Frequency Identity:**
   - E = ħω (Planck-Einstein)
   - E = mc² (Special relativity)
   - ∴ ħω = mc² → m = (ħ/c²)ω
   - In Planck units (ħ = c = G = 1): m = ω
   - Mass IS angular frequency — not an interpretation, algebraically inescapable
   - ZB frequency ω_ZB = 2mc²/ħ = 2ω_C
   - α as phase advance: α = re × ω_C / c

### Physical Foundations (Phase 3)
7. **Zitterbewegung parameters (Dirac equation):**

   - Source: Wikipedia (Zitterbewegung)
