# Chinese Mathematics: Liu Hui & Zu Chongzhi on π

**Task ID:** APH-1.9 | **Gap:** G6 (Historical Depth) | **Date:** 2026-07-17

## Liu Hui (劉徽, c. 225–295 CE)

### Context
Liu Hui's commentary (263 CE) on *The Nine Chapters on the Mathematical Art* (九章算術) transformed purely algorithmic Chinese mathematics into a discipline of mathematical reasoning.

### Liu Hui's π Computation
Using the inscribed polygon method (independently of Archimedes):
- 96-gon: π ≈ 3.14 (lower bound)
- 192-gon: π ≈ 3.1416 (or 3927/1250)
- Liu Hui recognized this as an approximation: "The more finely one divides, the smaller the remaining gap."

### Philosophical Stance
1. **Circle as limit of polygons:** Liu Hui explicitly described the inscribed polygon as approaching the circle in the limit — essentially the method of exhaustion
2. **Recognition of inexhaustibility:** "Division can continue without end" — proto-transcendental thinking, recognizing that π cannot be expressed as a finite rational number
3. **Algorithmic, not ontological:** Liu Hui described *how to compute π*, not *what π is*

## Zu Chongzhi (祖沖之, 429–500 CE)

### The Remarkable Approximation
Zu computed: **3.1415926 < π < 3.1415927** — precision not surpassed for nearly 1000 years (until al-Kashi, 1424 CE).

### Zu's Two Values
1. **"Inaccurate" (約率):** π ≈ 22/7 = 3.142857… (Archimedes' upper bound)
2. **"Accurate" (密率):** π ≈ 355/113 = 3.1415929… — the best rational approximation to π with denominator < 16604, accurate to 6 decimal places

### Analysis: Reification in Chinese Mathematics

- π was never named or symbolized
- Even Zu's high-precision values were presented as computational aids, not metaphysical truths
- Chinese mathematics did not develop the Platonic-realist framework
- However, distinguishing "accurate" from "inaccurate" implies a concept of an *ideal* value

### Conclusion: Intermediate Position
Chinese mathematics occupies an intermediate position in the reification spectrum: π was treated as a well-defined limiting quantity, but was never reified into an independent named entity. This supports the α-π-Helix thesis that reification is **culturally contingent, not mathematically inevitable.**
