# Sommerfeld's α: The Pre-History of the Fine-Structure Constant

**Task ID:** APH-1.11 | **Gap:** G6 (Historical Depth) | **Date:** 2026-07-17

## Before α: The Spectral Line Puzzle (1887–1913)

### Michelson & Morley's Fine Structure Discovery (1887)
Before α was a "fundamental constant," it was a **spectroscopic anomaly**. The hydrogen Hα line was not a single line but a close doublet — Δλ/λ ≈ 1/50000. The "fine structure" of spectral lines was unexplained by the Bohr model.

### The Balmer Series (1885)
1/λ = R_H (1/2² − 1/n²), where R_H is the Rydberg constant — purely empirical, no physical explanation.

### Rydberg's Generalization (1888)
1/λ = R (1/n₁² − 1/n₂²). R = 109737 cm⁻¹ became the "Rydberg constant" — another reified proportionality.

## The Bohr Model (1913): α Emerges

Bohr's quantization L = nħ gives electron orbital velocity v₁ = αc, where α = e²/(4πε₀ħc) appears naturally as the dimensionless coupling. Bohr did not name it.

The Bohr radius: a₀ = ħ/(α m_e c) — the Compton wavelength divided by α.

## Sommerfeld's Innovation (1915–1916)

Sommerfeld extended Bohr to relativistic kinematics. Energy expansion:

E_{n,j} ≈ −(m_e c² α²)/(2n²) [1 + (α²/n)(1/(j+1/2) − 3/(4n)) + …]

The fine-structure splitting ΔE ∝ α² is the first appearance of α as physically meaningful.

### The Naming of α (1916)
Sommerfeld introduced α in "Zur Quantentheorie der Spektrallinien":
α = 2πe²/(hc) = 1/137 — the "Feinstrukturkonstante" (fine-structure constant).

### The Value Puzzle
The near-integral value 1/137 spawned decades of numerological speculation. The α-π-Helix project's answer: 137 is not "special." α = r_e/ƛ is a geometric aspect ratio that happens to be approximately 1/137. The "mystery of 137" is itself a reification.
