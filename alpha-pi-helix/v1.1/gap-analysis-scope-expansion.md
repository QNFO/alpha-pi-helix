## Executive Summary

The α-π-Helix project has established a coherent geometric narrative — π, α, and mass as orthogonal projections of the Helical Compton Vortex — with Phases 0-7 complete and Phase 9 partially active. However, eight critical knowledge gaps prevent the thesis from achieving full scientific credibility. This analysis identifies 38 concrete research questions and proposes 34 new WBS entries across 8 gap categories.

**Headline finding:** The current thesis is geometrically elegant but **scientifically incomplete**. It does not demonstrate that this view produces better predictions than the standard model.

---

## Gap 1: Experimental Roadmap

The paper's central claim requires **unique measurable signatures** distinguishable from QFT. Current state: 1 experiment (APH-9.5 channeling), no timeline, no costs.

Research questions: polarized electron channeling, storage ring THz emission, attosecond laser scattering, e⁺e⁻ annihilation deviations, matter-wave interferometry.

Proposed: APH-10.1 to APH-10.5 (5 new tasks, Phase 10)

## Gap 2: Cosmological Connection

Zero cosmological content in the paper. If m = ω and α is a geometric aspect ratio, these are scale-invariant relations that should apply at cosmological scales.

Research questions: geometric scale hierarchy (Planck→Compton→Hubble), dark energy as horizon aspect ratio, EW phase transition vortex picture, baryon asymmetry from chirality, CMB anomalies.

Proposed: APH-10.6 to APH-10.10 (5 new tasks, Phase 10)

## Gap 3: Mathematical Rigor

No Lagrangian, action, or variational principle. No derivation of equations of motion. Analogous to Kepler's laws without Newton's.

Research questions: minimal geometric action, variational stability → α prediction, Noether conserved quantities, Dirac as effective theory, topological classification.

Proposed: APH-10.11 to APH-10.15 (5 new tasks, Phase 10)

## Gap 4: QFT Correspondence

No systematic comparison with QED precision observables. QED is the most precisely tested theory in physics.

Research questions: QED perturbative series as geometric series, muon g-2 prediction, Lamb shift from finite vortex extent, running α match, positronium spectroscopy.

Proposed: APH-10.16 to APH-10.20 (5 new tasks, Phase 10)

## Gap 5: Alternative Theories Comparison

No comparison with string theory, LQG, CDT, asymptotic safety, E8. Academic community will judge against established alternatives.

Proposed: APH-10.21 to APH-10.25 (5 new tasks, Phase 10)

## Gap 6: Historical Depth

Historical scope limited to Western European + Attic Greek. Babylonian, Egyptian, Chinese, Indian traditions need coverage. Sommerfeld's α pre-history incomplete.

Research questions: Babylonian/Egyptian treatment of circle ratio, Chinese (Liu Hui, Zu Chongzhi) treatment of π, Indian (Aryabhata, Madhava) infinite-series approach, Sommerfeld's α pre-history, reification case studies (k, h, G).

Proposed: APH-1.8 to APH-1.12 (5 new tasks, Phase 1 extension)

## Gap 7: Philosophy of Science

Reification critique is the philosophical backbone but lacks engagement with philosophy of science literature — structural realism, ontic structural realism, map/territory distinction.

Proposed: APH-10.26 to APH-10.30 (5 new tasks, Phase 10)

## Gap 8: Computational Validation

Most significant weakness: no novel numerical prediction. All geometric identities re-express known relationships.

Research questions: vortex stability simulation → α convergence, lepton mass ratios from topology, Penning trap corrections, Koide formula derivation, neutrino mass hierarchy.

Proposed: APH-9.16 to APH-9.19 (4 new tasks, Phase 9 extension)
